## The Two Doors

**Door A — /seat/**
Email capture. The lead magnet. Cold visitors give their email in exchange
for the free CEO Power Cheat Sheet and the Seat at the Table guide.
Primary campaign destination. Most posts point here.

**Door B — /welcome/**
Voice submission. Cold visitors tell us what a fair say means to them
before they commit to anything.
Used for posts with strong emotional or local resonance.

Never send cold traffic directly to /join/.

## Personal Engagement Protocol

**Within four hours of every post:**
Reply to every comment by first name, in Thomas's voice, one sentence.
Where the comment opens a door, end with a gentle pointer to /seat/ or /welcome/.

**Within twenty-four hours of every post:**
DM every share with a personal thank-you.
Reply to every direct message as a real conversation, not a transaction.

**Within forty-eight hours of every lead:**
Phone-number lead — personal text from Thomas's number:
"Hi, this is Thomas from COGS. Thanks for reading the guide.
Any questions, just text back. No pressure."

Email lead (not joined after three days) — personal follow-up from
ThomasC@cogsaustralia.org. Plain text. Five sentences maximum.

**Local leads — postcodes 2469, 2470, Clarence Valley, Northern Rivers:**
Flag for phone call or in-person follow-up at Drake Village Resource Centre.

## Compliance Rails

Check every post before it goes live.

1. Passes banned-framing check (no financial promotion, no party co-branding)
2. Disclaimer present where shares or dividends mentioned:
   "A community membership invitation. Not financial advice."
3. No party, candidate, journalist, or commentator named or implied
4. References cogsaustralia.org as the authoritative source
5. COGS not COG$ in user-facing copy
