The structural problem

Australians own the gas. Foreign-domiciled and offshore-institutional shareholders capture the dividend stream. The Australian government collects a small fraction of the export revenue through royalties and the Petroleum Resource Rent Tax — Treasury has confirmed that no offshore gas project has paid PRRT, and Santos paid its FY25 dividend 100% unfranked, evidencing minimal Australian company tax.

The result is that the Australian community has neither a meaningful share of the export revenue nor a structural voice in how the resources are extracted. Every previous instrument — from Gorton's AIDC to Rudd's RSPT — operated from outside the company and was defeated by the defensive machinery the industries deployed. The Foundation's strategy closes the gap from inside the shareholder register.

ESG improvement objectives

Before acquiring any shares, the Foundation publishes specific improvement objectives it will pursue at each company's AGM through Aggregate Unitholder Direction under clause 13A. These are durable shareholder priorities — not campaign demands — reviewed annually by the FNAC.

Santos Limited

First Nations
Independent annual reporting on Munupi/Tiwi, Narrabri ACHMP, Registered Aboriginal Party selection, and Yandruwandha Yawarrawarrka heritage standards.
Australian Taxation
Annual disclosure of Australian effective tax rate, PRRT position, and franking-account balance in ATO Tax Transparency format. Commit to franking from Australian-sourced earnings.
Emissions Transition
Annual say-on-climate vote with binding effect. Scope 1, 2 and 3 disclosure. Moomba CCS independent verification. Capital-allocation transparency.
Director Accountability
Annual director-by-director re-election vote. Executive remuneration linked to published ESG objectives. Post-employment lobbying disclosure.

Origin Energy Limited

First Nations
Annual reporting on Wakka Wakka, Iman, Mandandanji (APLNG) and Awabakal, Worimi, Wonnarua (Eraring transition) engagement. Queensland ACHA 2003 compliance transparency.
Retail Pricing Fairness
Independent annual disclosure of residential and small-business effective margin. Quarterly hardship-customer reporting. Disclosure of APLNG export volume and east-coast wholesale price relationship.
Transition Trajectory
Published Eraring closure timetable and renewable-replacement commissioning schedule. Capital-allocation transparency between fossil-extension and renewable-investment projects.
Director Accountability
As Santos, plus disclosure of APLNG-board representation conflicts and any director's prior gas-industry consulting, lobbying, or concurrent board engagements.

The two-instrument philosophy

The Foundation's Members Asset Pool holds three positions that together express a coherent philosophy of community resource stewardship applied across two materially different stages of the resource lifecycle.

Where extraction is not yet committed — steward the in-ground value. Where extraction is already committed — claim the community share of the value capture and direct engagement toward higher standards.

In-ground minerals retain real, measurable economic value as appreciating stewardship assets, recognisable without extraction and potentially in lieu of extraction. The shares give the community a legal voice; the in-ground resources carry real value that belongs to the community regardless of what is extracted.
