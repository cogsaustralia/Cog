UX Audit · /intro/ — warm-traffic gateway

A friend sent them. The page acts like a stranger.

COG$ of Australia
intro/ · 6 pages
Companion to: vault-audit, hub-audit
Reading level: K–6 (Year 5–6)

The visitor arrives at /intro/ because someone they trust sent the link. A daughter, a brother, the bloke at the pub. They are warm — but the warmth is borrowed, not earned. The page has roughly fifteen seconds to either honour the introducer's credit or burn it. Below: every place the language, layout, defaults, or sequencing fails that test, page-by-page across the six files in intro/.

This audit is read at a Year 5–6 reading level (K–6) on purpose. The page sells a community membership to every Australian. Every Australian includes a 16-year-old at a kitchen table, a 71-year-old on a Nokia, and a tradie reading on the bus between jobs. If a Year-5 child cannot say what each card is about after reading it once, the card is broken.

22 — Summary

Warm traffic is not just cold traffic with the friction turned down. It needs a different page.

The visitor at /intro/?code=COGS-XXXXX arrived because someone in their life — sister, mate, mum — clicked share. That introducer staked a small piece of their reputation. The job of /intro/ is to cash in the warmth: identify the introducer up front, mirror what the introducer probably said, and get to "join" before the warmth cools. The current page does the opposite. It buries the inviter's name in an async API call (so the strip pops in late, or never), runs five carousel cards of philosophical positioning, and only mentions the price on the final button.

The five cards are well-written for someone who has already decided. They are not written for someone who came in on a friend's recommendation and is asking three questions in this order: (1) Did Sarah really send this? (2) Is this a scam? (3) What is it actually going to cost me? Card 1 doesn't answer (1) reliably (the strip is async). Card 1 doesn't answer (3) at all (the price is in a pill but reads as one of four pills). Card 5 finally answers (3) — by which point a non-trivial share of warm visitors have closed the tab.

AudienceWarm Australian sent here by a friend. Has not researched. Trusts the friend, not yet the page.
Reading level (current)~ Year 9–10 in places (Card 3 "CHESS-registered", Card 4 "soulbound / financialised").

Warm-traffic principle

A page for warm traffic should feel like the friend is still in the room. The first thing on screen should be "Sarah sent you. Here's why she thought you'd want this." — not "COG$", a step counter, a skip button, and an ABN strip in 9-point grey.

23 — Priorities

If you only fix three things this week.

Fix now · Trust-critical
  1. Render the inviter strip server-side, not via fetch. Right now "Sarah thinks you deserve a say" only appears after a successful POST to /_app/api/index.php?route=invitations/validate. On slow mobile, the warm signal arrives second-paint or never. The single most valuable pixel on the page is rendered as an empty green box. Inject the inviter's first name from the URL code at request time, before the page is sent.
  2. Remove the hard-coded FOUNDER_FALLBACK_CODE = 'COGS-FUHT2L'. Every cold visitor without a code is currently being attributed to the founder by default, with a comment in the source saying "REMOVE AFTER 72 HOURS." This is in production. It will skew Pass-the-Coin attribution, the Founding Members ledger, and any incentive maths. Ship a removal commit before any other change.
  3. Put the price in Card 1, not on the Card 5 button. The button label "Secure my place. $4 once only ›" is the first time most users see the cost is locked in now. By Year-6 reading level, "Secure my place" parses as "save my spot for later." Hoist the $4 into the Card 1 callout in plain English: "It costs $4, once. We don't take it until you say yes on the next page."
Fix next · Reading level
  1. Replace "soulbound" everywhere it appears. Card 4 uses it without definition. A Year-6 child will read it as a fantasy-novel word. Use "yours forever and only yours" or "it stays in your name and cannot be sold".
  2. Replace "financialised" with a Year-6 sentence. Card 4: "The system cannot be financialised." Rewrite: "Nobody can buy the votes. The tokens cannot be sold for money."
  3. Define "Foundation Day" and "Expansion Day" in Card 5 in one sentence each. Same finding as Vault audit; same rewrite. Don't make the reader figure out the difference from the timeline icons (📅 vs ⛓️).
Fix later · Voice flow
  1. Stop asking the brand-new Member for a video testimonial as their second screen. voice-welcome.html currently sits one click after payment. The user has no story yet — they have a receipt. Move it into the Hub as an evergreen prompt.
  2. Fix the consent default on text submissions. voice-text.html shows "Shown as: your first name, your state" with a "Change" link — the disclosure is opt-out, not opt-in. State-level location should be a tick, not a default.
  3. Don't auto-submit the Santos / Origin canvass on first click. voice-confirmed.html POSTs the moment a sentiment button is tapped. There is no review step and no undo. Add a confirmation row.
Fix later · Sequencing
  1. Cut the carousel from 5 cards to 3. Cards 2 and 4 say similar things in different words ("one vote" / "you're an operator, not a passenger"). Merge Cards 2+4 into one card titled "What you actually get for $4".
  2. Move "Questions first →" out of the topbar and into the bottom of Card 1. Right now the topbar has two escape hatches (Skip + Questions first) styled almost identically. The cold-funnel audit had the same finding for the Vault topbar — the same fix applies here.
  3. Replace localStorage cogs_intro_seen with a session flag, not a permanent one. Once a visitor sees the intro, they never see it again on the same browser, even years later. A returning warm visitor (different campaign, different inviter) skips straight to /join/ with no re-orientation.
24 — First viewport

What the warm visitor's eye does in the first eight seconds.

Eye-tracking on referral landings is brutal: warm visitors look for the friend first, then the price, then the promise. /intro/ answers them in this order: wordmark, step counter, two dismiss buttons, ABN strip, card-1 number badge, heading, deck, four pills, callout. The friend is supposed to be in the green strip above the number badge — but the strip is display:none until the API call resolves.

24.1
Critical

The inviter strip is async — the warmest pixel on the page paints last

The single sentence the warm visitor came to see — "Sarah thinks you deserve a say" — only appears after fetch(/api/invitations/validate) resolves. On a 4G phone in a regional area that is 600–1500ms after first paint. By that time the user has already scrolled or read the heading. The strip is also display:none by default and only flips to display:flex on success — so any fetch error means the warm signal never appears.

intro/index.html line 354: document.getElementById('intro-inviter-strip').style.display = 'flex'; — only after data.valid === true && data.inviter_name.

FixServer-render the inviter strip from the URL code at request time. The HTML arrives with the name already in it. The fetch becomes a confirmation, not a reveal.
24.2
Critical

Two dismiss buttons in the topbar, styled the same

The topbar shows, left to right: wordmark · "1 of 5" · "Questions first →" · "Skip intro →". The latter two are both grey, both have arrows, both sit in the same row, both pull the user away from the content. A warm visitor reads them as "escape hatch" and "escape hatch." "Questions first" is actually a useful detour to a Skeptic's Guide. "Skip intro" is a finalising action that sets localStorage.cogs_intro_seen = '1' forever. They should not look identical.

intro/index.html lines 481–489: both buttons share color: var(--muted), background:none, border:none, ~0.78–0.82rem.

FixDemote "Skip intro" to a small text link in the bottom-bar, beside "Back". Promote "Questions first?" to a labelled link inside Card 1 ("Want the long version first? Read the Skeptic's Guide.") with no permanent skip side-effect.
24.3
High

The trust-anchor strip is illegible

Above the topbar, in 0.62rem (≈9.9px) at 32% opacity on a near-black background, runs: "COG$ of Australia Foundation · ABN 91 341 497 529 · Drake Village NSW". This is the legal identity of the entity asking for $4. It fails WCAG contrast and is below the 12px legibility floor on every device.

intro/index.html lines 67–77: font-size: .62rem; color: rgba(240,225,196,.32); pointer-events: none; — and explicitly non-interactive.

FixMove the ABN to the bottom-bar next to "Back", at 0.78rem and 60% opacity, as a focusable link to the registered-charity page. Take it off the top, where it competes with the warm signal.
24.4
High

"1 of 5" is a deflating first signal for warm traffic

For a cold visitor, "1 of 5" is reassuring: there's structure, a known length. For a warm visitor who landed here from a one-line text from their sister, "1 of 5" reads as "this is going to take a while." Combined with the two skip buttons immediately to its right, the topbar's strongest message is "there's a lot here, and you can leave."

FixReplace "1 of 5" with the inviter strip when an inviter is present. The progress count belongs inside the bottom-bar (next to Back / Next), not in the trust-establishing band.
24.5
Medium

The wordmark is a clickable home link with no affordance

"COG$" in the top-left is an <a> linking to / (with the partner code preserved). It looks like a static brand mark — no underline, no hover signal in the rendered CSS. Warm visitors don't expect the brand to be a way out, so they don't click it; cold visitors who do click lose their place in the carousel without warning.

FixEither kill the link (the wordmark is decorative on a 5-card carousel) or add a tiny "Home" label beside it. Don't ship invisible navigation.
24.6
Warm-only

The Foundation Day countdown chip only shows in the last 30 days

Lines 374–384 of index.html: "daysLeft >= 1 && daysLeft <= 30". Outside that window the warm visitor has no urgency cue at all — and a warm visitor 60 days out is still a person whose friend just shared a once-in-a-foundation moment. The chip should appear earlier (90 days?) and shift copy as the date approaches.

FixShow "Foundation Day · 14 May 2026" always (no countdown), and add a "X days away" suffix when 90 or fewer.

30 — Carousel mechanics

Skip, back, swipe, returning visitors — what the JS actually does.

30.1
Critical

localStorage.cogs_intro_seen is set permanently on first run

On any "Next past Card 5" or any "Skip", the code writes localStorage.setItem('cogs_intro_seen', '1') and on next visit redirects straight to /join/. This means: a warm visitor who opens the link, reads two cards, gets distracted, comes back tomorrow — never sees the carousel again, even though they never finished it. Cookie-clear / private-window behaviour is fine; routine return-visit behaviour is broken.

intro/index.html lines 695–704: if (localStorage.getItem('cogs_intro_seen') === '1') { window.location.replace(destination()); return; }

FixSet cogs_intro_seen only on (a) successful join completion (read the flag from the join page) or (b) explicit "Skip intro" — not on "Next past Card 5" alone. On return, default to Card 1 unless the user pressed Skip the previous time.
30.2
High

The "Skip" button has the same finalising side-effect as completing the carousel

Both complete() and skip() set cogs_intro_seen = '1' and route to /join/. From the visitor's perspective, "Skip" reads as "I don't want this right now" — but the code reads it as "I am committing to having seen this." The two should not be equivalent.

Fix"Skip" should set a session flag (sessionStorage), not a permanent one. Returning visitors get the carousel again. "Complete the join" is the only event that should permanently mark the intro as seen.
30.3
High

Swipe distance is 48px — too low; bounces while reading on a phone

Lines 757–765: if (Math.abs(dx) > 48) registers as a swipe. On a phone, that's a typical scroll-correction motion. A warm visitor reading Card 3 with a finger nudge slips forward to Card 4 mid-paragraph. The default carousel libraries use 80–120px for this reason.

FixRaise the swipe threshold to 96px and add a vertical-bias check (only register as a horizontal swipe when |dx| > 1.5 * |dy|).
30.4
High
