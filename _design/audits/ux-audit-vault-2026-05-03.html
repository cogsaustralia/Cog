01 — Summary

The vault is a competent fintech dashboard for an audience that did not sign up for a fintech dashboard.

The cold funnel sells a story: own a slice of Australia, one equal vote, $4 in. The vault delivers that story wrapped in vocabulary the user has never heard before — Sub-Trust A/B/C, Class S / Class C / Class D / Class kS / Class Lr / Class PIF, Personal S-NFT, Cryptographic DB, Independence Vault, Expansion Day, Foundation Day, GNAF zone verification, Distribution Election, JVPA. None of it is defined where the user first meets it. All of it appears in the first viewport.

Underneath the jargon the actual set of things a Member can do is small and reasonable: see what they have, change reservations, vote on polls, send/receive Community COG$, give to community, update their profile. The audit's central recommendation is a renaming and re-grouping pass, not a rebuild. The page below treats reading level as the primary lens because the cold-funnel audience does not have the vocabulary the vault assumes.

Reading level (current)~ Year 11–12 / business prose. Heavy nominalisation, passive voice, undefined acronyms.
Target reading levelYear 5–6 (K–6). Active voice, short sentences, every term defined on first use or replaced.
AudienceCold Australian who paid $4 ten minutes ago. No prior crypto, governance or trust-law literacy assumed.
If a Year-6 child reads the first viewport aloud and cannot say what this page is for, it is broken.

Right now the splash says "You are entering your Member Vault", the topbar says "Member Vault — COG$ Foundation", the sidebar shows "Cryptographic DB · Active" and "Foundation Day 14 May 2026", and the action banner asks them to "Complete your governance record to vote on 14 May." A child reads all of that and learns nothing about what the page does.

02 — Priorities

If you only fix three things this week.

Fix now · Comprehension-critical
  1. Replace "Vault" with "Your COGS account." The word vault appears in the splash, the topbar, the wallet route, and at least four info tooltips. It signals safe-deposit-box / crypto-wallet, both of which raise anxiety. Account is what a member came for.
  2. Replace every token-class code with a plain noun on first surface. "Class C · Transferable now" should read "Community COG$ — you can send these to other members today." Keep the technical class codes only inside an "Advanced details" disclosure.
  3. Define "Expansion Day" and "Foundation Day" once, at the top, in one sentence each. Both dates are dropped on the user with no context: the sidebar shows "Foundation Day 14 May 2026" and the reservations card says "Released after Expansion Day." A new member cannot tell if these are 11 days away, 11 years away, or the same event.
Fix next · Trust-critical
  1. Demote the "governance record" address-of-birth banner. Within seconds of paying $4 the user is asked for their street, suburb, state and date of birth in a gold-bordered banner labelled "Action required." This reads as a second hidden ID check after the join form. Move it to a profile setup step with a why we ask line.
  2. Reconcile the price, again. Same finding as the funnel audit: the pay modal still shows "$4.00 membership contribution" at the top and "$4.40 by card via Stripe" at the bottom. A member who has just paid $4 by bank transfer might see this modal and panic.
  3. Show the entity name and ABN in the topbar or footer of the vault. Currently the only legal identification visible is the words "COG$ of Australia Foundation" in the page title. ABN, registered address, and a contact link belong on every authenticated page, not just on a marketing landing.
Fix later · Polish & depth
  1. Collapse the seven-tab top nav. Overview already summarises Holdings, Exchange and Give; the dedicated tabs duplicate. Three tabs will do: My COG$, Community (polls + news), Activity & profile.
  2. Drop "kS-NFT", "S-NFT", "Lr", "PIF" and similar shorthand from user-facing copy. Use them only in advanced disclosures. Replace with Kids COG$, Member token, Resident COG$, Pay-it-forward.
  3. Add a one-screen "What is this place?" on first-ever vault open. 4 cards, 60 seconds. After that, never show again. Currently first-run is identical to every other run.
Watch · Risk register
  1. "Cryptographic DB · Active" status pip in the sidebar. This is the kind of phrase that journalists screenshot. It implies blockchain. If the system is a Postgres database with hashes, say so privately and remove the phrase from the UI.
  2. "Distribution election" toggle (AUD bank vs. new ASX COG$). Soliciting a future-securities preference inside a $4 community account is the single most likely ASIC/securities trigger. Hide until Expansion Day or behind explicit opt-in.
  3. "Cryptographically receipted" voting copy. If votes are stored in the same Postgres as everything else, this overstates the system. Soften to "each vote is logged with a one-way receipt — the tally cannot show who voted for what."
02b — /wallets/ entry point

The "wallets index" is a 246-byte redirect. That is the finding.

The URL /wallets/ resolves to wallets/index.html, which is six lines of HTML containing nothing but <meta http-equiv="refresh" content="0;url=member.html">. Anyone — bookmarked link, shared URL, returning member, search engine, screen reader — gets bounced into the Member Vault before the page renders. There is no router, no auth gate at this layer, no choice screen between member / business wallets (despite wallets/business.html existing as a sibling).

Three concrete consequences for first impressions:

2b.01
Critical

Meta-refresh from /wallets/ straight into the authenticated vault.

A meta-refresh fires before any auth check — the user sees the vault chrome flash even when they are not logged in. On a flaky connection, screen readers announce "Redirecting…" then "Member Vault — you are entering your Member Vault" with no time to read either. The page also has noindex,nofollow,noarchive, so any shared /wallets/ link is a dead end for previewers.

Source: wallets/index.html · 246 bytes · <meta http-equiv="refresh" content="0;url=member.html">
FixReplace with a server-side redirect (301/302) when authenticated, and a real "Pick your wallet — Member · Business" chooser when not. Never rely on meta-refresh into authenticated chrome.
2b.02
High

No member/business chooser despite two distinct wallet apps existing.

wallets/business.html is 108 KB — a fully separate experience — but the /wallets/ URL hard-codes the member redirect. A business member who lands on /wallets/ gets the wrong app and has to navigate out.

FixEither route by user type on the server, or show a 2-card chooser at /wallets/: "Personal account" / "Business account."
2b.03
Medium

The redirect's fallback link reads "your vault."

The visible HTML for users whose meta-refresh fails (older browsers, strict privacy modes, screen readers) is "Redirecting to your vault…" Same vault-vs-account language problem as the rest of the audit, in the very first thing a slow-connection user sees.

FixRewrite the fallback as "Loading your COGS account…" with a real link.
02c — Visual UX / UI

What the page looks like, before you read a word of it.

The previous sections audited the language. This one ignores language entirely and looks only at visual signals — colour, hierarchy, density, motion, weight — that fire in the first second a new member sees the page. Those signals shape "do I trust this?" before the reader even parses a sentence.

Palette readPure-black #080808 ground · gold #e8b84b accent · white text at 88% / 55% · serif italic numerals.
Genre signalHigh-end crypto exchange / private banking. Bloomberg-meets-Binance. Not "community fund."
MismatchCold funnel sells community, $4, one equal vote. Vault visually screams seven-figure portfolio.
2c.01
Critical

The visual register contradicts the brand promise.

The vault is dark mode + gold gradient + serif italic numerals + spinning coin splash + "vault" framing. Every cue is borrowed from premium-finance UI (Revolut Metal, Coinbase Pro, private banking apps). The cold funnel positioned COGS as community-owned, one equal vote, $4-in. When a member who paid $4 lands in a UI that visually says "high-net-worth crypto holdings", the dissonance reads as either a bait-and-switch or a scam dressed up.

Evidence: --bg:#080808, gold-on-black gradients on every CTA, vaultEntrySpin coin animation on splash, serif italic for all balances at 2.2rem.
FixLight-mode default with restrained warm neutrals; reserve dark+gold for an opt-in "Pro" view if it must exist. Match the visual register to the funnel: warm, civic, plain. The funnel and the vault should look like the same product.
2c.02
Critical

Gold is overloaded — it means everything, so it means nothing.

Gold is used for: the brand wordmark, every primary CTA, the topbar pills on hover, all monetary balances, the splash text, the "unread" rail dot on news, the active tab underline, the kpi top-line gradients, the chip on Reservations, the action-required banner border, the kpi values, the editable input borders, the inline-edit save button, the kS-NFT row icon, the "I've sent payment" notify button. A user has no way to tell which of these gold elements is actually clickable, urgent, financial, or decorative.

17+ distinct gold treatments visible in the first viewport: --gold, --gold-bg, --gold-bdr, gold linear-gradients, gold pulsing dots, gold underlines.
FixGold = action only (one CTA per region, max). Use neutral surface treatments for status, brand, balances. Status colours should be functional (green=ok, amber=attention, red=problem), not decorative gold.
2c.03
High

The splash animation is the loudest motion on the page and runs every visit.

A coin spins for ~1.6 seconds with a 0–360° Y-axis rotate, drop-shadow glow, then fade-in lines reading "Member Vault — You are entering your Member Vault." Every page load. The animation is heavy on the senses, makes the product feel ceremonial, and delays first interaction. For a returning user opening the app for the 50th time, it is a tax. For a new user, it sets a tone the rest of the product cannot live up to.

FixShow splash once (first ever load), then never again — or replace with a 200ms fade. Treat the vault like an app, not a temple.
2c.04
High

Serif italic numerals at 2.2rem make every balance look like fine-print prose.

Balances use the Cormorant Garamond serif at 2.2rem, italic-leaning, gold-on-black, with negative letter-spacing. Numbers in financial UIs should be tabular monospace or geometric sans so digits line up and scan fast. The current treatment optimises for "elegant magazine spread," not for "is my balance correct." It also makes — (loading state) and 0 visually similar at a glance.

.kpi-val, .tr-amt, #xch-balance-val, #give-hero-val all use serif weight 500 with letter-spacing -.02em.
FixTabular-figures sans-serif for all numbers (font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums). Reserve the serif for headings and editorial copy, not balances.
2c.05
High

Hover-translateY(-2px) on six different elements creates a jittery first interaction.

Every primary button, every topbar pill, every tab, every tour pill, every inline-edit, every "send payment" notify button lifts on hover with a 2px translate plus a 6px gold shadow. Mousing across the topbar makes the entire row of pills bob in sequence. It reads as theme-park, not as a bank.

FixRemove the translateY across the board. Restrict hover state to colour/border change. Save motion for genuinely interactive transitions (panel open, modal slide).
2c.06
High

Topbar wastes its real estate on three icon-pills before the page identifies itself.

Top of the screen, left to right: small coin logo, the words "Member Vault" in gold serif, the user's name, the user's member number in monospace, then ⚙ Profile / 🏠 Members Hub / ↩ Log out. A new member sees no entity name, no ABN, no support contact, no "what is this product." The most prominent actions in the topbar are leave (Members Hub) and log out.

FixTopbar left: full entity name + ABN microcopy. Topbar right: a single "Account" menu containing profile, settings, support, log-out. Reduce to a single dropdown.
2c.07
High

Three competing top-level nav surfaces stack within 100px of vertical space.

From the top: (1) topbar with brand and pills, (2) tab nav fixed below it with 7 horizontal tabs, (3) inside Overview, a "section divider" that styles like another tab strip ("Community COG$", "COG$ Reservations", "Management Hubs"). Each looks navigable. The tab nav and the in-page section dividers are visually almost identical (gold underline, uppercase tracking, same height). A new user cannot tell whether scrolling moves them through Overview content or whether they need to click another tab.

FixDifferentiate the section dividers with completely different styling — left-aligned serif headlines at 1.4rem, no underline, no gold. Tab nav and section headers should not look like siblings.
2c.08
High

The "Action required" governance banner uses the same gold-on-navy treatment as a high-stakes financial alert.

The banner asking for street/suburb/state/DOB sits at the top of Overview in linear-gradient(135deg,#1a1a2e,#16213e) with a gold border and an amber CTA. This treatment is reserved in most financial UIs for "unauthorized transaction detected" or "verify identity to prevent account closure." Using it for a profile-completion ask creates manufactured urgency around a low-stakes task.

FixDemote to a calm light card with a neutral border. No urgency styling. Place inside a profile setup checklist, not floating at the top of Overview.
2c.09
Medium

Body text is locked at 1.02rem across almost every element.

Card subtitles, table descriptions, inline notes, footer chips, button labels, tooltips, form labels, helper microcopy — almost all sit at 1.02rem. There is no visual hierarchy between "this is a major instruction" and "this is a 0.78em footnote." The page reads as a wall of equally-weighted small text broken only by serif balance numerals.

26 occurrences of font-size:1.02rem across the stylesheet.
FixBuild a 4-step type scale: 13/15/18/24px. Use one and only one size per role. Body 15, helper 13, section title 18, card hero 24.
2c.10
Medium

Chip soup: 8+ chip variants, no system.

Chips for status (paid/unpaid), chip for "Released after Expansion Day", chip for unread news count, chip for tab badge, chip for invite count, chip for governance "Action required," chip for "No fees" / "$0.40 fee," chip for "Class C" type identifiers. Each is its own colour pairing — gold/amber/blue/green/dim — with overlapping semantics. A new user cannot decode "is this colour telling me good, bad, or neutral?"

FixThree chip families only: status (green/amber/red), category (one neutral grey), numeric badge (one accent). Document and enforce.
2c.11
Medium

The KPI cards have a 1px gold gradient line at the top of every tile.

It is decorative — it carries no meaning, no state, no CTA. Combined with the gold balance number inside, the gold border on the parent card, and the gold tab underline above, the page becomes a stack of horizontal gold lines competing for attention.

FixRemove. If a "premium feel" is desired, do it through type and spacing, not decorative borders.
2c.12
Medium

The "Independence Vault" / "Cryptographic DB · Active" footer chip is decorative chrome that sets off alarms.

It sits in the sidebar foot, flanked by a green dot and the date "Foundation Day 14 May 2026." Pure cosmetic — but it is the only piece of dynamic-looking signal in the rail. Users will read it as "this is the system status I should monitor." If the underlying DB is Postgres, calling it "Cryptographic DB" in user-facing UI is unwise.

FixRemove. Or replace with "All systems normal" linking to a real status page.
2c.13
Medium

Empty / loading states show — everywhere instead of meaningful zero-state.

Community COG$ balance, reservations, kids count, give hero, exchange address — all show — until JS resolves. A new member with $0 of CC and no reservations sees a page full of dashes that look like errors.

FixUse real zero states: "You don't have any Community COG$ yet — you can earn or buy them after Foundation Day." Skeleton shimmers (already partly implemented) for genuine loading.
2c.14
Medium

"i" / "?" tooltip buttons are the same size as text, and there are 22 of them in the first viewport.

Tooltips at the same visual weight as the heading they sit beside create constant visual noise — the eye tracks each one. They also signal "this product is too complex to explain inline."

FixInline the explanation in body copy. Keep "?" only for second-level legal text. When used, render as a 12px neutral icon, not a 1.02rem button.
2c.15
Nit

The brand wordmark uses the dollar sign in "COG$".

It is on-brand for the project but visually screams crypto-token-ticker. In a vault that asks for $4 of community money, the dollar-sign-as-letter-S reinforces the trading-platform misread. Use "COGS" in the topbar wordmark; reserve "COG$" for the token unit name.

FixTopbar wordmark: COGS. Token references in body copy: COG$. Different roles, different glyphs.

If a new member screenshots this page and sends it to a friend, the friend's first guess is "is this a crypto exchange?" or "is this a private bank?" — never "is this a community fund I should join?" That is the visual gap to close.

03 — First 60 seconds

What a brand-new member sees, in order.

Reconstructed from the live DOM as it loads for an unverified, just-paid member:

  1. Splash: "Member Vault / You are entering your Member Vault" over a coin animation.
  2. Topbar: "Member Vault — COG$ Foundation", their name, their member number in monospace, and three pills: Profile · Members Hub · Log out.
  3. Sidebar with three section headers — Financial, COGS Governance, Community — eight nav items, and a footer chip reading "Cryptographic DB · Active · Foundation Day 14 May 2026."
  4. A gold-bordered banner: "Action required — Complete your governance record to vote on 14 May." Asks for street, suburb, state, date of birth.
  5. Below that: their name and member number again, a "paid/unpaid" chip, an "Outstanding payments" card if any orders are pending.
  6. Then the Community COG$ card showing a balance of — (loading or zero).
  7. Then a COG$ Reservations card pre-loaded with three rows the member never asked for (ASX COG$ / RWA COG$ / Landholder COG$) with the caption "Released after Expansion Day."
  8. Then a Mainspring Hub link, then an invite card.

That is roughly nine distinct pieces of information, four undefined acronyms, two undefined dates, one demand for personal details, and no welcome message. The user has not yet been told what they own, what to do next, or where help is.

3.01
Critical

The first-run experience does not exist.

A returning member and a brand-new member see exactly the same page. There is no "welcome — here is what just happened" panel. The single most important moment in the entire product is treated as just another page load.

No first-run flag · no localStorage.firstRun check · splash text identical for run #1 and run #1000.
FixOne-time welcome card at the top of Overview for the first 7 days: "You are now a Member of COGS. You hold one equal vote and one Member token. Here is what you can do today."
3.02
Critical

Date-of-birth and address ask, served as "action required."

Within seconds of arriving the user is told to enter street, suburb, state, and date of birth to be allowed to vote. There is no preamble, no "why we ask," no privacy link, and no option to do this later. The framing implies the $4 they just paid did not actually buy them voting rights.

Element #governance-banner · gold-on-navy · CTA: "Save & complete →" · failure message styled as red error.
FixMove into a calm "Profile · finish setup" card. Lead with one sentence: "To make sure each vote comes from a real person in Australia, we need to confirm your address and birthday. We never share these." Make dismissable.
3.03
High

Two member-number readouts in the first 200px.

The topbar shows the member number in monospace. The Overview "status strip" shows it again immediately below. New members do not yet know what a member number is for, and seeing it twice in two lines makes them think the second one is a different field.

FixShow member number once, in the topbar, with a "copy" action and a one-line tooltip: "This is your unique COGS ID. You'll quote it for payments and contact."
3.04
High

"Cryptographic DB · Active" footer chip.

Sits in the sidebar, no tooltip, no link. Implies blockchain to a crypto-literate user, implies "this is technical and possibly risky" to everyone else. There is no user need that this status pip serves.

FixRemove. If a status indicator is needed, replace with a green dot and the words "All systems normal." Link to a status page if one exists.
3.05
Medium

"Foundation Day 14 May 2026" with no context.

Reads like an event invitation. Could be: a launch date, a vote date, a deadline, an anniversary. The user cannot tell, and the date appears in three places with three different contexts (sidebar, governance banner, reservation card).

FixPick one canonical phrase and use it everywhere: "First national vote — 14 May 2026" if that is what it is. Add an info tooltip that defines it once.
04 — Navigation & IA

Seven tabs and a sidebar are too many doors for a $4 product.

The current vault has both a desktop sidebar (8 items, grouped under "Financial / Governance / Community") and a horizontal tab nav (7 tabs). They duplicate each other. New members do not know which one drives the page.

4.01
High

Two parallel navigation systems for the same destinations.

Sidebar items and top-tab items overlap (Overview, Holdings/Reservations, Exchange, Polls, News, Give, Activity). Pick one. A vault this small does not need both.

FixKeep the top tabs. Drop the sidebar entirely on mobile (it already hides) and on desktop (replace with a left-aligned breadcrumb or simply nothing).
4.02
High

Tab labels mix metaphors: "COG$ Reservations," "COG$ Management," "COG$ News."

Three tabs starting with "COG$" make scanning slow. The labels are also internally inconsistent: Management means polls (governance), not management of holdings; Reservations means future tokens; News means notices.

FixRename to plain verbs/nouns: My tokens, Polls, Updates, Send & receive, Give, History. Drop the COG$ prefix from tab labels.
4.03
Medium

"Members Hub" and "Member Vault" are two different places, both linked from the topbar.

The topbar pill labelled "🏠 Members Hub" takes the user out of the vault entirely (to /partners/index.html). New members will not know which is the home page for them.

FixPick one home. If Members Hub is the canonical landing post-login, send the user there first and link into the vault from there. Don't put both in the same toolbar with the house icon on the external one.
4.04
Medium

Hidden info-buttons (?) carry critical definitions.

The most important plain-English definitions in the product live inside small ? tooltips: what is Community COG$, what is the Independence Vault, what is the distribution election. A user who does not click them never sees the explanation.

22 instances of iq-btn tooltips found in wallets/member.html · all default to collapsed.
FixSurface the one-line plain-English version inline next to the heading, in regular body text. Keep the ? only for second-level legal/technical detail.
4.05
Nit

Sidebar icons use unicode glyphs (◈ ◇ ⇄ ⬡ ✦ ◉ ♡ ◈).

"Overview" and "Activity" share the same glyph (◈). The diamond, hexagon, and lozenge variants (◇ ⬡ ✦) are not visually distinguishable at small sizes.

FixUse a real icon font (Lucide / Phosphor) or no icons at all. Never reuse a glyph for two destinations.

5.01
