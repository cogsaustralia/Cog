01 — Summary

The funnel reads as a community pitch but behaves like a fintech.

Every page is dark, gold, italic-serif, and emotionally charged. That tone is fine for a manifesto. The problem is the asks sitting underneath the manifesto are a financial-product sequence: email → mobile → personal details → $4 → invitation code → bank-transfer details → a "vault" with 2FA → a Joint Venture Agreement. A cold Australian who has heard of COGS for the first time five minutes ago is being asked to do something that looks materially like buying a security from a stranger overseas, on a brand they do not recognise, with no proof of organisation behind it.

The single highest-leverage change is not visual. It is making the $4 ask honest about what it is on the page that asks for it, and making the identity of the receiving entity obvious before the form. Everything else is downstream of that.

Step 1 · Seat
Get the guide
Email only · soft ask
Leaks: weak proof, no logo, claim-density high
Step 2 · Inside
Read + give mobile
Long-form guide · phone capture · $4 CTA
Leaks: SMS ask before value paid off
Step 3 · Join
Pay $4
Name, email, mobile · invite code · "register"
Leaks: hidden caretaker code; no payment yet
Step 4 · Thank-you
Set up vault & pay
Open vault · Stripe $4.40 · or bank transfer $4
Leaks: surprise fee, second sign-up, JVPA later

The user is told the price is $4 four times before page 4, then on page 4 sees $4.40. They are told it is "one step" on page 1, and end up doing a multi-stage signup with a vault password and 2FA.

02 — Priorities

If you only fix three things this week.

Fix now · Conversion-critical
  1. Reconcile the price. Do not advertise $4 and then charge $4.40. Either eat the Stripe fee, advertise "$4 + 40c card fee" everywhere, or default to bank transfer with card as a clearly-labelled alternative.
  2. Move payment into /join/. Submitting the form should either take payment or clearly say "no money yet — payment is on the next screen, takes 30 seconds." Right now you collect identity then send the user to a separate "vault setup" before payment.
  3. Stop the silent invite-code injection. A hidden COGS-FUHT2L caretaker code is auto-applied for cold visitors. Either make the cold path truly cold-friendly with no code field, or show the field and explain why it is pre-filled.
Fix next · Trust-critical
  1. Above the fold of /seat/: identity proof. Add the registered entity name, ABN/ACN, and a 1-line plain-English statement of what COGS is. Currently the only proof is "Drake Village NSW" in tiny footer text.
  2. Show JVPA before the form, not after. Asking for legal acceptance after the user has paid is a dark pattern in spirit even if technically legal. Surface a 4-bullet plain-English summary on /join/ with a link to the full document.
  3. Drop the second mobile-capture on /seat/inside/ — you already have the email; asking for SMS halfway through reading the guide reads as bait-and-switch on "just your email."
Fix later · Polish & risk
  1. Tone down the dark-gold "wealth product" aesthetic — it actively conflicts with "ordinary Australians, $4."
  2. Replace scrollIntoView with explicit scroll to avoid layout shift; remove the unused 6-step field-stepper that pretends to be a multi-screen form.
  3. Add an exit-intent / soft-save on /join/ step 1 so a half-completed form is not lost.
  4. Audit copy for unverified-sounding claims ("five thousand Members", "First Nations Advisory Council with binding authority") — either evidence them on-page or soften.
Fix later · Accessibility
  1. Gold-on-near-black body copy is around 4.5:1 at best on key surfaces and below on muted text. Lift --text2 and --muted, or darken behind them.
  2. Form errors are signalled only with red border colour. Add inline text errors with an icon and an aria-live region on every field, not just one global status.
  3. The auto-validating invite code (and silent caretaker code) creates a status change with no announced text — add aria-live.
1
The cold landing
/seat/index.html
Above-the-fold first impression

Headline is strong ("How one share makes the boss listen.") and the single-field email form is right. But on first contact with a cold Australian:

— There is no logo, no entity name, no organisational proof in the visible viewport. The header is the wordmark COG$ at 1.05rem. That is not enough trust currency for the claim being made.

— The dollar-sign brand mark COG$ is the strongest visual signal on the page and it reads as money product, which fights the message "no money, just your email."

— "Free guide" eyebrow lands but is buried; the trust statement "No spam. No subscription. One email." below the input is the most important reassurance and should be louder.

1.1
High

No identifiable organisation above the fold

A cold visitor cannot answer "who is this?" without scrolling. The byline "I am Thomas" appears below the fold; the entity "COG$ of Australia Foundation" appears only in the footer.

evidence: seat/index.html:127 footer-only entity ref
FixAdd a small logo + "COGS of Australia Foundation · ABN xxx" line to the header. Optionally a 1-line "A community Foundation registered in NSW" under the headline.
1.2
High

Claim density without evidence

Three big legal claims appear below the fold ("three legal tools", "real say", "shareholders inside the meeting") with zero linked source, citation, or social proof. A skeptical reader has no anchor for trust.

FixAdd at least one anchor: a Corporations Act citation, a press mention, an ASIC company link, or a screenshot of a regulator filing.
1.3
Med

The dollar-sign mark fights the message

COG$ rendered in italic gold serif reads as a luxury financial product. The page text says the opposite ("no money, no catch"). The visual identity is undermining the copy.

FixEither commit to "ordinary Australians, $4" with a humbler palette/wordmark, or commit to "real shareholder power" with a more institutional palette. The split-brain is hurting both.
1.4
Med

Foundation Day "14 May 2026" is a ticking clock that no one has seen yet

Below the fold a band announces "The first community vote." Without context this reads as an ICO/launch date, which triggers the same skeptical pattern as crypto presale pages.

FixOne sentence: "This is when registered Members vote on the first governance resolution. Not a token launch."
1.5
Nit

Two visit-tracking beacons fire on every load

The seat page fires a track/visit pixel with p=seat; /join/ and /thank-you/ each have a second beacon block. Harmless functionally but worth consolidating.

evidence: seat/index.html:152
FixOne shared cogs-track.js; cleaner privacy posture too.
2
The guide + SMS gate
/seat/inside/index.html
Above-the-fold first impression

The user just gave their email. They expect "the guide." Instead the page opens with a confirmation band, then a long-form essay ("Seat at the Table"), and partway down the page asks for a mobile number to "send one text on Foundation Day."

This violates the page-1 promise of "One email." The SMS section is reasonably worded — but its existence on this page, before any value-exchange beyond "you are reading the guide", is the single biggest credibility break in the funnel for a skeptical Australian.

2.1
Crit

Mobile capture contradicts the landing-page promise

Page 1 says: "No subscription. One email." Page 2 asks for a phone number for an SMS. Even though it is opt-in and bounded ("one message"), users will read this as the bait. Trust loss compounds for the rest of the funnel.

evidence: seat/inside/index.html:54 phone-section
FixRemove this from the cold flow entirely. Move the SMS opt-in into the /join/ page or the post-join vault, where the user has already chosen to engage. Replace this section with a calendar add-to-cal for 14 May.
2.2
High

The guide reads like a manifesto, not a reference

Four numbered sections ("You get a vote at the AGM", "ESG laws put money on your voice"...) with no ToC, no scannable summary, italic pull-quotes every 200 words. A cold visitor on mobile will not finish.

FixAdd a 4-bullet TL;DR at the top, collapse each section behind a clickable summary, and put the join CTA inside the page (sticky bottom bar) instead of only at the end.
2.3
High

"First Nations Advisory Council with binding authority" is a strong claim with no proof on page

This is a powerful differentiator if true, and a serious problem if it is overstated. Currently it is asserted in section 4 without naming the Council members, the governing-document clause, or the date of constitution.

FixEither link to the Council page with named members + the specific clause of the governing document, or remove "binding" until that page exists. Soft-claim ("a permanent advisory seat") is safer.
2.4
Med

The CTA at the bottom says "Join for $4 on Foundation Day"

That phrasing implies the user must wait until 14 May to join. Then the button takes them to /join/ right now, which works today. The mismatch is small but each one reads as carelessness, and this funnel cannot afford carelessness.

evidence: seat/inside/index.html:68
Fix"Reserve your seat for $4 — Foundation Day is 14 May 2026." Make the date a date, not a gate.
2.5
Med

Substack link in the cold flow leaks the user out

A "Read the CEO Power Cheat Sheet" Substack link sits next to the join CTA. A cold visitor who is half-convinced will tap the lower-commitment option (Substack), not the $4 button.

FixMove the Substack link to the footer or remove from the cold path. Reading material belongs after a yes, not as a competitor to it.
3
The $4 form
/join/index.html
Above-the-fold first impression

Strongest page in the funnel by structure: a clear two-pane layout, a "what you get" trust box, a 2-step progress indicator. But the conversion-critical issues are inside the form, not at the top.

— The hero says "Your $4. Your voice. Your vault." This is the first time the word vault appears in the funnel. A cold visitor does not know what a vault is, and the trust box uses jargon ("Independence Vault", "Member Polls", "cryptographic management system").

— The "Become a Member. $4" button works, but the price reassurance ($4 once only · no subscription · no hidden fees) should be one line under the button, not buried in the trust card.

3.1
Crit

Hidden caretaker invite code auto-applied for cold visitors

An end-of-page script silently sets invite_code_input.value = 'COGS-FUHT2L', hides the entire invite section, and validates it. If discovered (curl, view-source, a refunded user), this looks deceptive. If the audit team finds it, it looks like a hard-coded backdoor.

evidence: join/index.html:1188 caretaker default invite
FixFor cold visitors, do not show the invite section at all and do not silently inject. The server should accept "no code" for the cold path and assign attribution server-side. If a code must exist, it should be issued per-session with an audit trail, not hard-coded in HTML.
3.2
Crit

Price changes between this page and the next

The form is titled "Amount due today: $4.00." The thank-you page leads with "Pay $4.40 by card — processed securely via Stripe." A user who clicked because of $4 and lands on $4.40 will bounce, screenshot, and post.

evidence: thank-you/index.html:498 Stripe CTA
FixOne of: (a) absorb the 40c, (b) write "$4 + 40c card fee, or $4 fee-free by bank transfer" on every page from /seat/ onward, (c) make bank transfer the default and card the secondary option. Do not surprise.
3.3
Crit

"Register as a Member" submits without taking payment

The submit button creates a Member record and routes to thank-you. Payment ($4 / $4.40) is collected on the next page via Stripe link or bank transfer. The user has been led to believe submitting is joining; in fact they have to do a second separate action to actually be a Member.

evidence: thank-you/index.html "complete your $4 payment"
FixMove payment into the form. The button on step 2 should be "Pay $4 and join" with the Stripe element inline, or a clear two-stage button: "Reserve free now, pay $4 next." Do not leave the user thinking "did I just join, or did I just give my data?"
3.4
High

JVPA acceptance was moved out of the form

Comments in the source ("JVPA acceptance moved to vault post-login") confirm the user is being asked to accept a Joint Venture Partner Agreement after they have submitted personal details and paid. That is the wrong order legally and ethically.

evidence: join/index.html:649
FixShow the JVPA summary on the form. Three plain-English bullets (what you are joining, what your $4 is, what you can do later), one checkbox "I have read the Joint Venture Partner Agreement" with a real link to the PDF, and a "read full document" expander.
3.5
High

The 6-step "field stepper" is ceremony, not progress

First name → Middle name → Last name → Email → Mobile, one-at-a-time, with Next/Back. A cold visitor does not need a five-screen flow for five short fields they can see at a glance. It feels like a gamified onboarding for an app, on a page asking for $4.

evidence: join/index.html:546 field-stepper
FixShow all five fields at once. The 2-step (details → confirm) outer progress is fine; the inner stepper should go.
3.6
High

"Independence Vault" is undefined jargon at the moment of decision

The word "vault" carries every connotation the user is anxious about (crypto, custody, password recovery, "where my money is locked"). It is used four times above the fold without ever being defined. The FAQ link "What is the vault?" exists in the side panel — that means the team knows.

FixFirst mention should be: "Your Member dashboard (we call it the Independence Vault)". After that, "your dashboard" is fine. Save "vault" branding for inside the product.
3.7
Med

Long progress simulator looks like a stall

On submit the status cycles "Lodging your registration" → "Creating your Member record" → "Securing your reservation" → "Almost there" over ~3 seconds, regardless of actual response time.

evidence: join/index.html:986 _pMsgs sequence
FixShow one neutral spinner. Theatrical multi-stage progress on a 200ms request reads as fake, and on a real 5s slow request, as broken.
3.8
Med

Hidden "stewardship_module" answers are auto-filled at submit

The submit handler attaches a stewardship_module object with five hard-coded answers ("agree_understand", "long_term_stewardship", "fifty_fifty_split", "veto_extraction", "acknowledge_risk") the user never saw. If these answers are referenced elsewhere as "Member-attested", that is a misrepresentation.

evidence: join/index.html:1024
FixEither show the five questions on-screen (radio cards) and capture the actual answer, or remove the object entirely. Do not pre-attest.
3.9
Med

scrollIntoView is fragile and used in three places on submit/error

The starter docs warn against this; on smaller viewports it can scroll the page into a half-state.

FixUse window.scrollTo({ top: el.getBoundingClientRect().top + window.scrollY - 80, behavior:'smooth' }).
3.10
Nit

Confirm step says "Pay by bank transfer (no fee) or card (40c Stripe fee applies)"

Good — but this is the first mention of card fees in the funnel and it's inside a confirm row, two clicks past the headline price.

FixPromote this line to the cost row directly under the price.
4
The handoff
/thank-you/index.html
Above-the-fold first impression

"You're in" headline + member card is a strong post-conversion moment. But the page does five different jobs stacked vertically: confirm, ask for a public-facing voice statement, prompt invite-link sharing, set up vault, pay $4.40 (or $4 by bank), and read three "while you're here" cards. The user does not know which is required and which is optional.

4.1
Crit

Required-vs-optional steps are not visually distinguished

The user has just been told (on page 3) "Almost there. Hit submit. Your vault opens straight away." Then on this page they encounter (in order): confirmation card; a public-voice form; a "pass the coin" share panel; a vault CTA; a Stripe pay button; a bank-transfer accordion; three more cards. Nothing says "to actually be a Member, you must do step X."

FixOne required action at the top: Pay $4 to activate your membership. Everything else collapsed below. The voice submission, invite-share, and "while you're here" all belong on a follow-up page or post-pay screen.
4.2
Crit

Voice submission asks for public sharing immediately after registration

"In one sentence — why did you join?" with a checkbox: "I agree this can be shared on COG$ social media. My first name may be shown." The user has been a Member for ten seconds. Asking for a public-facing testimonial in the registration flow is dark-pattern-adjacent.

evidence: thank-you/index.html:312 vs-ty-panel
FixMove into the dashboard. If you want it on this page, gate it behind "Your $4 is paid" and make the share-permission opt-in, not bundled with submission.
4.3
High

Stripe CTA uses a different visual language than the rest of the site

Background linear-gradient(135deg, #1a1a2e 0%, #16213e 100%) and a different button style. It looks like an injected third-party widget — exactly the moment when the user needs the brand to feel cohesive.

evidence: thank-you/index.html:498
FixRestyle the Stripe block to match the gold/dark glass-card system. Same border, same radius, same button.
4.4
High

Bank transfer details are accordion-hidden behind a thin grey "▸" link

The cheaper, fee-free option is a collapsed details summary at color:#64748b. The more expensive Stripe option is the default. This is the wrong way around for a Foundation that just promised "no surprise charges."

FixDefault to bank transfer (with PayID highlighted), put Stripe as "Prefer to pay by card? +40c fee."
4.5
High

"Open my vault" before "Pay $4" creates orphan members

If a user clicks the gold primary CTA, they go to /partners/?welcome=1 to set a password and 2FA. They have not paid. The funnel-tracking code expects payment to follow registration; the UI lets the user wander off.

FixMake the primary CTA "Pay $4 to activate" and the secondary "Set up vault later". Or merge: pay first, then immediately set vault password on the same screen.
4.6
Med

Member number renders as "Loading…" then sometimes "Check your email"

If the API response or URL params do not carry member_number, the most prominent piece of social proof on the screen becomes a fallback message. A user who has just paid £4 of trust capital will read this as "something failed."

FixServer-render the member number into the redirect URL deterministically; show a dignified fallback like "Your Member ID is being generated — we'll email it within 60 seconds."
4.7
Med

Banned-phrase voice-submission filter could trigger false positives

The filter blocks investment, profit, ROI, presale, etc. Sensible. But a user writing "I joined because I want a return to community decision-making" will trip on returns?.

FixTighten the regex with word-boundary context (\binvestment\s+returns?\b) and show the warning as soft (yellow), not block.
07 — Cross-cutting

Issues that show up on every page.

X.1
High

Brand vs. message dissonance

Every page uses the same dark gold + Playfair italic combination that is the visual grammar of premium financial services. The product is "$4, ordinary Australians, community governance." Either one of those has to bend.

FixTest a humbler typographic system on a B-variant of /seat/: warm paper, dark ink, a sans body font, gold reserved for one accent. See if cold-visitor → guide conversion lifts.
X.2
High

Privacy posture is implicit

No page links to a privacy policy in a visible place; the seat page captures email, the inside page captures phone, the join page captures full name + DOB + address (hidden but populated). For a $4 community Foundation this is more data than the value exchange justifies.

FixVisible privacy link in every footer; on /seat/ a one-liner "We won't share your email. Privacy." next to the input.
X.3
Med

Acknowledgement of Country is a static footer line

"Wahlubal Country, Bundjalung Nation" appears in the footer of every page. Combined with the strong claim that First Nations Custodians have binding governance authority, this should be more than a sign-off line in 11px gold.

FixOne dedicated card on /seat/ below the fold that names the Council and links to a page explaining how the binding authority is enacted.
X.4
Med

Inconsistent terminology across pages

Member / Partner / S-NFT holder / Joint Venture Partner / Foundation Member appear interchangeably across the four files. The submit endpoint is snft-reserve; the form is "Become a Member"; the API returns "partner_id"; the next page is /partners/; the welcome heading says "Welcome, Member."

FixPick one user-facing word ("Member") and never expose the others. Rename the public route from /partners/ to /members/; keep "snft", "partner_id", "JVP" as internal-only.
X.5
Med

No exit / abandonment recovery

Email is captured on page 1 and stored in localStorage as cogs_lead. There is no system that emails a user who got to /join/ and did not submit, or submitted but did not pay.

FixOne-shot recovery email at +24h to anyone with a cogs_lead who never converted. Same content as /seat/inside/'s TL;DR + a single CTA.
X.6
Nit

Mobile breakpoints partial

On /join/, the trust-box stacks below the form on narrow viewports — fine. But on /thank-you/ the involve-grid drops to 1 column at 560px while the member-card stays at 520px max-width, creating an awkward in-between zone around 640–760px.

FixAdd a 640px breakpoint that stacks the involve-grid earlier.
