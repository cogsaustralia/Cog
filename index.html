Why Now?

The resources of Australia are currently controlled and profited from by publicly listed corporations. COG$ addresses this through community stewardship, ownership, governance, benefits and profit distribution.


The Community Resources Wealth Trust Foundation — a not‑for‑profit foundation — is accumulating ASX shares in Australian resource companies and creating community‑led governance and stewardship. Dividends and benefits issued directly to members and communities.


Instead of companies or institutions owning ALL the nation’s resource wealth, the community itself becomes a legal steward as a shareholder, influencing governance, voting at company AGMs and sharing directly in the profits generated by Australia’s resource economy.

Why This Has Never Existed Before

Every sovereign wealth fund in history has been created and controlled by governments. Examples include Norway’s Sovereign wealth fund and Australia’s Future Fund. While these funds manage enormous national wealth, the people whose land and resources produced that wealth rarely hold a direct share in the assets themselves and have little or no say in its operations or governance.


COG$ introduces a different model. Instead of government ownership and corporate control, the community becomes a governing shareholder. Members participate directly in governance and share in the stewardship and benefits generated by Australia’s natural resources. Sometimes there is more value leaving resources where they are, in the he ground.

How the Trust Structure Works

The Community Resources Wealth Foundation Trust (NFP) holds ASX-listed resource company shares on behalf of the community. Every share is registered directly through the ASX CHESS system in the Foundation’s name.


Members participate through their personal NFT COG$ tokens that represent governance participation and COG$ provide dividend rights. The Trust cannot be privately owned, sold, or captured because its rules are locked in the founding deed and governed by your votes.


This structure ensures that the underlying assets remain permanently community-owned while governance remains democratic and transparent.

More COG$ Participation Options

Beyond the Personal NFT COG$ $4 Aud membership, COG$ allows several additional participation paths that help accelerate the growth of community ownership.

Investment COG$

Investment COG$ are additional COG$ tokens that members may reserve beyond their Personal NFT COG$ membership token. Each COG$ token is priced at $4 Aud.


These tokens increase your proportional participation in the dividend distribution but do not provide additional governance votes. Governance remains one‑member‑one‑vote to protect democratic control. More money doesnt equal more power.


By allowing members to reserve additional tokens, the community can accelerate the capital used to accumulate ASX resource company shares.

Landholder COG$

Landholders may reserve COG$ tokens linked to their land at a rate of up to 10,000 tokens per hectare, (or any part thereof - less than 1 hectare = 10,000 COG$ max) @ $4 Aud per COG$ token.


This connects surface land ownership with the mineral and resource wealth beneath it. Landholder tokens participate in dividends while also enabling weighted local governance of resource use, mining and exploration decisions affecting your local region.


When projects relate to specific land areas or mining tenements, local landholder members receive additional weighted voting input on those project decisions. This ensures that the communities living on the land where resources are extracted have a stronger voice in how those projects proceed.


This model aligns the interests of communities living on resource land with the companies extracting those resources.

Donation/Philanthropic COG$

Supporters may also contribute through Philanthropic donation COG$ tokens @ $4 Aud. These contributions increase the number of shares the Foundation can acquire without increasing the voting power of any single member. 25% of dividends are reinvested in more ASX listed shares, 50% is used for Foundation operational expenses and 25% is invested in community led imitiatives


COG$ Donations help grow the community asset base faster and allow individuals or organisations to support the project’s long‑term goal of community resource ownership.

Why These Accelerate Growth
