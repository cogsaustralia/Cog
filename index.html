A message from the project coordinator

For generations, the natural wealth of Australia has flowed primarily to corporations and governments. The people whose communities sit above these resources rarely hold direct ownership, and have little to no say in the operations of companies extracting those resources — or whether those resources get extracted at all.

COG$ allows the community to become stewards of their local resources and environment. Through the Community Resources Wealth Foundation Trust, members collectively accumulate ownership, stewardship, and legally enforceable governance of Australian resource companies. We participate directly in governance and share in any realised profit.

The goal is not short-term speculation or a meaningless gesture. It is the creation of a permanent community governance and wealth structure that compounds across days, years, and generations.

COG$ started with a simple step: people are now reserving their place, and communities are demonstrating collective support ahead of Foundation Day, 14 May.

Why now

The imbalance is structural. So is our solution.

Every sovereign wealth fund in history has been created and controlled by governments. Norway's Sovereign Wealth Fund. Australia's Future Fund. While they manage enormous national wealth, the people whose land and resources produce that wealth hold no direct share in the assets and have little say in the decisions.

COG$ introduces a different model. Instead of government ownership and corporate control, the community becomes a governing shareholder. Members participate directly in governance via the Foundation and share in the stewardship and benefits generated by Australia's natural resources.

Foundation structure

A not-for-profit Trust — locked for the community.

The Community Resources Wealth Foundation Trust is a not-for-profit entity. Its rules are locked in the founding Trust Deed. The Foundation cannot be privately owned, sold, or captured.

Locked Trust Deed

The Foundation's rules are permanently encoded in the Trust Deed. No individual, corporation, or government can alter the community-first governance structure.

No private ownership

The underlying assets remain permanently community-owned. No shareholder, director, or institution can claim ownership of Foundation assets for private gain.

Democratic governance

Governance is one member, one vote. More financial contribution does not buy more power. The community always governs the Foundation, not its largest funders.

CHESS-registered shares

Every share is held directly on the ASX CHESS register in the Foundation's name — in trust for community beneficiaries. No fund managers, no intermediaries, no hidden layers.

Seat of Country in perpetual trust

The Foundation is permanently tied to the land — a Seat of Country is held in perpetual trust, anchoring the community mission in the Australian landscape itself.

Non-profit distribution model

50% of dividends go directly to members. 50% are reinvested to compound community stewardship. No profit is extracted for corporate or personal gain outside of membership entitlements.

Governance model

Community power.
Structurally protected.

Major governance decisions remain democratic across the entire community. When resource extraction or exploration decisions affect specific land areas, local landholders receive additional weighted voting influence — ensuring the communities living on the land have the strongest voice.

National decisions: One member, one vote. Every personal COG$ NFT holder has an equal say in Foundation-wide decisions regardless of financial contribution.

Local land decisions: When a project impacts a specific region, local landholder members receive additional weighted votes based on their Landholder COG$ — your land affected means more say than anyone else.

AGM voting rights: The Foundation exercises its shareholder rights at company Annual General Meetings — a legally enshrined platform to influence resource company decisions at board level.

Wealth cannot capture governance: A $4M maximum per person on investment COG$ ensures no individual or institution can buy disproportionate influence over the Foundation's direction.

First acquisition target

Legacy Minerals — Drake, NSW.

The Foundation's first target is Legacy Minerals (ASX: LGM), which controls approximately 9,000 km² of mineral tenements across New South Wales — including gold, silver, copper, zinc, and nickel independently JORC-valued at $5.9 billion, with a current market cap under $40 million.

These tenements include many communities, tens of thousands of people, and a diverse range of unique ecosystems, habitats, and waterways — including areas around the Clarence River, Timbara River, and the township of Drake, NSW. Without local stewardship and legitimate community involvement, the impacts of traditional operations and extraction are historically evident in these areas.

Their flagship project, Mt Carrington at Drake (NSW), is currently free of farm-in agreements or joint ventures — making it a suitable foundation asset for the Foundation's community stewardship pilot.

The long-term goal is to gradually accumulate stewardship and governance across multiple ASX-listed resource companies, giving communities real economic participation and legally enshrined governance over the use of Australia's resources.

Importantly, underground assets may potentially remain in the ground while still being monetised — there are successful global precedents for this approach.

Frequently asked questions

Common questions.

Is my $4 at risk?
No payment is made during the reservation period. On Foundation Day (14 May), you choose whether to proceed. Your $4 membership fee goes into the Foundation's community trust structure — it is not a speculative investment but a one-time governance membership. The $4 is not a financial product or share purchase.
What happens if Foundation Day is delayed?
If regulatory requirements or other circumstances require Foundation Day to be rescheduled, all reservation holders will be notified directly by email. Your reservation simply carries forward — there is no obligation and no penalty.
What are the tax implications for members?
The ATO Class Rulings process currently underway will determine the exact tax treatment of COG$ membership fees, dividend receipts, and token transactions. All rulings will be published in full to reservation holders before Foundation Day so you can make an informed decision.
Is COG$ a financial product or investment?
The classification of COG$ tokens is precisely what the ASIC Innovation Hub engagement is determining. No tokens are issued today. The reservation process creates no financial product, involves no payment, and establishes no legal financial relationship. We will only proceed when regulatory classification is clear and compliant.
Who governs the Foundation?
The Foundation is governed by its members through democratic voting. The Trust Deed locks this structure permanently — no single person, company, or institution can alter the governance model. A founding committee oversees establishment, but ongoing governance transfers fully to the member community once the Foundation is operational.
What if I own land near a tenement area?
Landholders can reserve Landholder COG$ tokens linked to their land at up to 10,000 tokens per hectare. This gives you weighted voting rights on resource decisions directly affecting your region — more than the standard one-member-one-vote for national decisions. Your land being affected means your voice carries additional weight in those specific decisions.
How does the Foundation handle my personal data?
Personal information collected during reservations is held securely and used solely for the purpose of the COG$ Foundation reservation and membership process. A full Privacy Policy compliant with the Australian Privacy Act 1988 will be published before Foundation Day. You may request deletion of your reservation data at any time by contacting the project team.
Where can I read the whitepaper?
The COG$ White and Black Paper is in final preparation and will be published ahead of Foundation Day. You can request an advance copy by noting your interest in the reservation form below. It will cover the full legal structure, Trust Deed framework, regulatory status, financial model, and governance design.
