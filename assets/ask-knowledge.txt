Q: How is this different from a charity?
A: The Foundation is a not-for-profit hybrid trust, not a registered charity. While it has community benefit purposes (Sub-Trust C), it also holds real assets (Sub-Trust A) and distributes income to members (Sub-Trust B). A charity typically cannot distribute income to individual members. The Foundation's structure combines civic governance with economic participation — members have both a voice and a stake in the value the Foundation holds.

Q: Which instrument governs the Foundation?
A: The Declaration is the principal operative and governing instrument. It governs the Hybrid Trust and Beneficiary Holder rights, and prevails over any CLG constitution or other constituent document. The Corporations Act still prevails where required by law. The public Black and White Paper is a constitutional companion that explains the deed in public language — it does not replace the deed.

Q: Is the Foundation automatically a CLG plus trust structure?
A: No. The Declaration starts with the Hybrid Trust under an initial individual trustee and treats a Board of individual trustees as the default structure. A CLG or other corporate trustee is only brought in later if members validly resolve to adopt one or if applicable law requires it.

Q: What exactly is the no-fiat rule?
A: It is a fundamental, entrenched condition that minted tokens must never be sold or traded for fiat currency. This stops the project drifting into speculative behaviour. A prohibited fiat transaction is void ab initio. The prohibition covers selling tokens for AUD, listing on any exchange, or converting to cash. The rule cannot be altered by any board or member resolution.

Q: Are any fiat-side flows still allowed?
A: Yes, but not as secondary cash trading of tokens. The Declaration still uses fiat-denominated issue prices, requires fiat distributions through Sub-Trust B where applicable, sends $2 of each Donation COG$ issue price directly to Sub-Trust C, and fixes 25% of the Donation Dividend Stream for trustee administration.

Q: Can Investment or Landholder tokens be transferred?
A: Yes, but under class-specific rules and only after the twelve-month Lock Period. ASX COG$ may then be gifted or exchanged for goods or services with another KYC-verified Beneficiary Holder at an agreed non-fiat value, and eligible holders may convert their own ASX COG$ one-for-one into Landholder COG$ or Donation COG$. Landholder COG$ may be transferred only to another eligible Landholder Beneficiary Holder on a gift or goods-and-services basis, has a specific property-title transfer election when the underlying land changes hands. All permitted dealings run through the Foundation Community Vault with required authorisations.

Q: Can I help someone else get a membership?
A: Yes. The Pay It Forward pathway lets members fund memberships for others, including people experiencing hardship or documentation barriers. Before allocation, Pay It Forward does not carry a personal beneficial interest or governance right. On allocation, the funded membership is issued as the relevant recipient class and the Pay It Forward entry is cancelled.

Q: What is the Foundation Community Vault?
A: The Foundation-operated multi-signature blockchain wallet and smart-contract system used for minting, registry, geo-fencing, eligibility verification, transfer controls, and other controlled token operations. Core operations require the blockchain-registered cryptographic authorisation of at least three Board Directors. For Community Basket holdings the CHESS Register remains the primary legal record of ownership.

Q: Is the Foundation Community Vault the same as a personal wallet?
A: No. The Foundation Community Vault is the Foundation-side control layer. The Independence Vault and B-NFT Business Vault are member-facing interfaces and do not replace it.

Q: Is blockchain always the definitive legal record?
A: Not in every respect. For Community Basket holdings, the CHESS Register is the primary legal record and the on-chain RWA record is a supplementary transparency layer. Where the Declaration expressly gives on-chain records priority over an off-chain member register, that specific rule still applies. For holdings data, any discrepancy is resolved in favour of the CHESS record.

Q: What blockchain stack is planned?
A: A planned controlled, permissioned EVM architecture: Hyperledger Besu first as the governance and compliance core, Ethereum mainnet later for public proof anchoring, and Base later as a member-facing UX layer rather than the constitutional source of truth.

Q: What role do AI agents play?
A: AI agents are assistants for preparation, support, routing, monitoring, verification, drafting, reconciliation, and explanation. They are not autonomous final decision-makers over member rights, token issuance, wallet recovery, or governance outcomes.

Q: Does the structure require a separate CLG annual meeting?
A: Only if a CLG or other corporate trustee is later adopted. The Declaration presently requires the Initial Trustee to convene the Inaugural Meeting within two years, with trust-governance resolutions then following the Declaration. A separate company AGM becomes relevant only if and when a corporate trustee structure actually exists.

Q: What is the Skeptic's Guide?
A: The Skeptic's Guide is a dedicated page on the COG$ website that answers the hard questions plainly — regulatory standing, supply controls, governance protections, the no-fiat rule, and what $4 actually gets you. It is designed for people who want to scrutinise the structure before joining. It addresses five core questions: where is the value, is it a crypto scam, can the rules be changed, what does $4 get you right now, and what is the actual regulatory standing. It also explains the RWA COG$ 100M supply cap, the founding phase notice, the starting participation positions, and the two-tier token structure. Available at /skeptic/.

Q: Is COG$ a managed investment scheme or financial product?
A: Governance-tier membership tokens (S-NFT, kS-NFT, B-NFT) do not require AFSL or MIS registration. They are governance memberships, not financial products. Investment-linked token classes (ASX COG$, RWA COG$, Landholder COG$) may constitute financial products under the Corporations Act and will not be issued until full AFSL authorisation, ASIC MIS registration, a compliant Product Disclosure Statement, and ATO clearance are each in place. The Foundation is progressing this compliance through the ASIC Innovation Hub. The $4 membership fee is not a payment for a financial product.

Q: What is the AFSL pathway status?
A: No AFSL licence has yet been applied for or granted. The Foundation is engaged with the ASIC Innovation Hub, which is the formal precursor pathway through which the Foundation is working to understand and satisfy the requirements before a formal AFSL application is lodged. This is an active, transparent process. Members are informed of its progress in real time through the Independence Vault. Investment Foundation Day — when Tier 2 investment-linked tokens can be issued — requires full AFSL authorisation, ASIC MIS registration, a compliant PDS, and ATO clearance. These conditions are mandatory and cannot be waived by any resolution of members or the board.
